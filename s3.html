PROCEDURE FOR BIRTH CERTIFICATE

1.To apply for a Birth Certificate, you must first register the birth with “panchayat/ municipal/Corporation / Health Department” in your area.
2.Link for contact information: Corporations in Tamil Nadu AND Chennai corporation
3.The birth has to be registered with the concerned local authorities within 21 days of its occurrence, by filling up the form prescribed by the registering authority or as per the given link below along with a certificate from the person or Medical Institution, who conducted delivery in the case of birth.
4.Beyond the prescribed time limit,

1.21 to 30 Days – with penalty of Rs.100
2.30 days to 1 year - written permission of the Officer prescribed and on payment of late fee of Rs. 200/-.
3.After 1 year - Any birth which has not been registered within one year of its occurrence shall be registered by an order of the Executive Magistrate not below the rank of a Revenue Divisional Officer and on payment of late fee of Rs. 500/-.


5.Link for form: Form1
6.Normally the dispensaries or hospitals where the birth takes place maintain a register and the same is sent to the local bodies at regular intervals for registering the records.
7.Either way, it should be registered first.
8.To get the birth certificate, one has to approach the authority and apply for the certificate on any working day.
9.Applicant shall use the form as per the link to apply on his own: Form 0
10.Once the form along with documents were submitted, Authorities will accept the form, Check the register for records by verifying the details and documents to issue the certificate.
