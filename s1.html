CONDITIONS FOR COMMUNITY CERTIFICATE

Community Certificate Tamil Nadu Community certificate is an important document issued by the Revenue Department to certify that a person belongs to a particular reserved community like Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe, and other Backward Classes. Community certificate issued by the concerned State Governments are also known as caste certificate. Community certificate is mandatory for obtaining benefits in education and employment sectors from the Government. In this article, we look at procedure for obtaining community certificate in Tamil Nadu.

Eligibility

The following are the eligibility criteria for a person to apply for a community certificate.
1. The applicant should belong to any reserved category such as Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe or Other Backward Classes.
2. The applicant should be an Indian citizen.
3. The applicant should be residing in Tamil Nadu.
4. The applicant should have completed 3 years of age.

Documents Required

The following documents are necessary to apply for a Community Certificate.
1.Filled application of the Community Certificate. 

2.Parents’ identity proof and an affidavit stating the applicant’s name, 
     a)father’s name, 


      b)address, and community; if the particular applicant is a minor.

3.Address Proof
4.Ration Card
5.Proof for the age of the applicant
6.Caste Certificate of the parent
7.Aadhar Card

1. Community Certificates can either be applied online or offline.
